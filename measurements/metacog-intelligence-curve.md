## Two Knowledge Types (discovered 04/03)

Compile #24 introduced a second promotion pathway:

| Type | Signal | Threshold | Count | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Patterns** | Frequency | n>=3 occurrences | 38 | "entry-only-regime-gating" |
| **Phase transitions** | Impact | n=1 via topical influence | 12 | "overlay-architecture-events-not-regimes" |

Patterns are branches — recurring reasoning structures validated by repetition. Phase transitions are forks — single events that reorganized downstream knowledge. Instance count is the wrong signal for conceptual breakthroughs; topical influence measures structural impact regardless of frequency.

## The Intelligence Curve

The compile cycle exhibits diminishing returns on PATTERN promotion (most common patterns found by compile #15) but NOT on phase transitions (forks can appear at any time). The system's knowledge growth has two regimes:

1. **Early growth (compiles 1-15):** Rapid pattern discovery. 5→30 promoted patterns. Low-hanging fruit.
2. **Mature growth (compiles 15-25):** Patterns plateau. Phase transitions become the primary discovery mechanism. The scaffold's reasoning doesn't get "more" patterns — it gets "deeper" forks.

This mirrors biological cognitive development: procedural skills plateau while conceptual understanding continues to deepen.

## Implications

- The metacognitive compile cycle produces measurable, monotonic growth in promoted patterns
- Two distinct knowledge types require two promotion mechanisms
- The system's intelligence curve has a plateau in frequency-based patterns but not in impact-based forks
- Each promoted pattern is a reusable reasoning template that measurably improves agent performance (R14: +18.5% with pattern priming)
